The Revolving Internet

This piece of work is part of a series of browser-based artworks by Constant Dullaart. It incorporates an animated interface of the Google search page with the intention to draw attention to the infrastructure of mega corporations that have been involved in the many users’ experiences of the internet. Taking the interface as a medium, its functionality is retained but with the continuous rotation, the experience of using the search page becomes increasingly absurd - not only was browsing difficult, it becomes hypnotic and nauseating. Dullaart's attempts to prompt users to consider their reliance on such platforms.

"I was trying to see if I could use code to spin the entire page around. In video art that was kind of the ultimate thing to do–to have like this 360-degree turn of the camera. I felt like that would be this ultimate thing. I could make this vision of this new world turn again."

High Retention, Slow Delivery

“High Retention, Slow Delivery” was launched with the aim to examine the mechanisms of control and the hierarchies of social media. It is an online performance by Dullaart where he purchased 2.5 million fake instagram followers, then distributing them amongst a selected group of people in the art-world, granting them the coveted 100,000 milestone on social media and at the same time “equalizing” these accounts.

“Audience is a commodity. They can be used to influence politics, by supporting political causes online, and even add relevance to art.”
